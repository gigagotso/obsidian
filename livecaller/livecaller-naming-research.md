# LiveCaller Product Suite — Naming Research

A suite of four platforms under the **LiveCaller** brand (Zoho-style). Three are business tools unifiable in a single dashboard; the fourth is a standalone consumer messenger. This document defines what we're naming, lays out the main **philosophies of naming** with trade-offs, applies each to the products, and records the conflict checks done so far.

> ⚠️ **Scope of the conflict checks:** the review at the bottom covers **existing businesses and products** (web search, June 2026), **not** live `.com` availability — a registrar can't be queried here. Before committing to any name, confirm:
> - **Domains** at instantdomainsearch.com or Namecheap
> - **Trademarks** at **USPTO** (US), **EUIPO** (EU), and **Sakpatenti / საქპატენტი** (Georgia)
>
> Trademark and Georgian-language clearance are critical given the suite's bank, government, and enterprise clients.

## 1. What we're naming

Four platforms. Products 1–3 are **business tools** that run standalone or combine into one dashboard. Product 4 is a **standalone consumer messenger** — a different kind of beast.

| # | Platform | What it is | Faces | Tone it should signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Contact Center** | Omnichannel customer communication — calls, live chat, email and social integrations; multichannel agent workspace | Customers (external) | Reach, reliability, responsiveness |
| 2 | **Project & Task Management** | Internal work, task, and project tracking | Teams (internal) | Momentum, structure, clarity |
| 3 | **Team Chat** | Internal Slack-style messaging | Teams (internal) | Closeness, speed, collaboration |
| 4 | **E2E Encrypted Messenger** | Standalone consumer messenger, WhatsApp/Telegram-style, end-to-end encrypted | Public (external) | Privacy, security, trust |

**Two different naming problems here:**

- **The business suite (1–3)** shares **one dashboard**, so its three names get read *together*, side by side. They need a coherent logic — a literal family resemblance or at least a consistent feel. A jarring mix will look accidental in a unified UI.
- **The consumer messenger (4)** is a public, mass-market app competing with WhatsApp and Telegram. It lives on its own — on app stores, in consumers' hands — so it can (and arguably *should*) carry a more **independent, standalone brand** than the business tools. Consumer messengers win on a short, memorable, friendly-but-trustworthy name; the LiveCaller endorsement matters far less to a consumer than to a procurement officer.

**Audience note:** for the business suite, primary buyers are **enterprises, government, and banks**, with the **Georgian market** as home turf. These reward names signalling *trust and credibility* over *playful disruption*. The consumer messenger's audience is broader and more emotional — its name can be warmer and punchier, while still signalling security.

## 2. Philosophies of naming

There's no single "right" way to name — each philosophy optimizes for something different. Here are the main approaches, what they buy you, and what they cost.

### A. Descriptive / Functional
The name states what the product does. *e.g. LiveCaller Connect, LiveCaller Tasks, LiveCaller Chat.*

- **Strengths:** instantly clear; great for SEO; bulletproof to trademark **as a set** under the LiveCaller mark; zero learning curve for buyers.
- **Weaknesses:** generic; hard to trademark as standalone words; forgettable; little emotional pull.
- **Best when:** trust and clarity beat distinctiveness — which often describes bank/government procurement.

### B. Suggestive / Evocative
A real word that *hints* at the benefit without describing it literally. *e.g. Pulse, Relay, Forge, Cadence.*

- **Strengths:** memorable, emotive, still meaningful; balances clarity and brand.
- **Weaknesses:** the good ones are mostly **taken** in SaaS; trademark conflicts common.
- **Best when:** you want personality but can survive a harder clearance search.

### C. Coined / Invented (neologism)
A made-up word, often built from fragments or sounds. *e.g. Privo, Tracta, Konvo, Krypta.*

- **Strengths:** highest chance of a **free domain and clean trademark**; fully ownable; scales across languages.
- **Weaknesses:** starts as an empty vessel — needs marketing spend to acquire meaning; risk of sounding generic-startuppy.
- **Best when:** you're building a long-lived brand family and want defensible IP. **This is usually the safest path in saturated categories.**

### D. Metaphorical / Lexical (real word, abstract use)
An unrelated real word borrowed for its associations. *e.g. Onyx, Roost, Kiln, Harbor.*

- **Strengths:** evocative and human; easy to say and spell.
- **Weaknesses:** very crowded in SaaS; the obvious metaphors (vault, cipher, cloak, haven) are already gone in the security space.
- **Best when:** you find an unexpected, uncrowded word with the right feel.

### E. Brand-extension / Family ("branded house")
Tie each product to the LiveCaller parent, usually via a shared element. *e.g. the "Live-" prefix: Livedesk, Liveboard, Livechat — or LiveCaller [X].*

- **Strengths:** maximum cohesion; every product markets the parent; trivial to extend with a 4th, 5th product.
- **Weaknesses:** the "Live-" namespace is **already crowded** (Livespace, Liveflow, Livepools all taken); products can feel interchangeable; weak standalone identity.
- **Best when:** the suite *is* the product and you want the master brand to dominate — a strong fit for a single-dashboard story.

### F. Compound / Portmanteau
Fuse two ideas into one word. *e.g. Taskflow, Chatline, Convera.*

- **Strengths:** descriptive *and* brandable; self-explaining.
- **Weaknesses:** many obvious combos are taken; can feel clunky.
- **Best when:** you want a hint of meaning baked into a coined-ish word.

### G. Classical roots (Latin / Greek)
Draw on Latin or Greek stems for gravitas. *e.g. Loqui (to speak), Nuntio (messenger), Opus (work), Concord, Agora.*

- **Strengths:** signals **trust, permanence, seriousness** — well-suited to banks and government; feels established, not faddish.
- **Weaknesses:** can read corporate/cold; popular stems are heavily mined.
- **Best when:** credibility with conservative, regulated buyers is the priority.

### H. Local / Cultural (Georgian roots)
Build on Georgian language and heritage. *e.g. ხმა (khma, "voice"), ხიდი (khidi, "bridge"), წრე (tsre, "circle").*

- **Strengths:** **distinctive globally** and resonant at home; signals sovereignty and local commitment — a real asset with Georgian government and bank clients; likely uncrowded internationally.
- **Weaknesses:** pronunciation/spelling friction in export markets; needs native-speaker validation for unintended meanings and clean transliteration.
- **Best when:** the Georgian market and public-sector trust are central to positioning. **This is your most ownable, hardest-to-copy angle — worth serious exploration.**

## 3. Brand architecture — how the three names relate

You weren't sure on this, so here are the three models and a recommendation given the single-dashboard reality.

| Model | What it looks like | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Branded house** (unified) | *LiveCaller Connect / Projects / Chat*, or a shared root | Strongest cohesion; one brand to build; cheap to extend | Products lack standalone identity; "Live-" space crowded |
| **House of brands** (independent) | Three unrelated standalone names; LiveCaller in the background | Each product can target its own market; flexible | Expensive — you're funding three brands; weak suite story; worst fit for one dashboard |
| **Hybrid / endorsed** (sub-brands) | Distinct names that share a consistent *logic or feel*, endorsed by "by LiveCaller" | Personality **and** cohesion; reads as a deliberate set | Requires naming discipline to keep the family coherent |

**Recommendation — split the decision:**

- **Business suite (1–3):** Given one shared dashboard, conservative bank/government buyers, and the crowded "Live-" namespace, a **hybrid / endorsed model** is the sweet spot — three coherent coined or culturally-rooted names, each carrying "by LiveCaller." If you'd rather minimize risk and spend, a **branded house** with descriptive names is the bulletproof fallback.
- **Consumer messenger (4):** lean toward a more **independent (house-of-brands) name**. It competes in a consumer category where a strong, standalone identity beats a corporate endorsement, and where being visibly part of a B2B vendor can even work against mass adoption. Keep a light "by LiveCaller" footprint for credibility, but let the messenger stand on its own name.

## 5. Conflict check — results so far

This is the original clearance work. The "secure messaging" items map to **Platform 4 (E2E Encrypted Messenger)**.

| Name | Originally for | Status | What's already out there |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Livepools** | Team chat | ❌ TAKEN | Indian fantasy-sports software company (acquired by i3 Interactive). Owns livepools.com |
| **Livespace** | Project mgmt | ❌ TAKEN | Established Polish B2B CRM, 1000+ clients (livespace.io). Also a US AV company |
| **Liveflow** | Project mgmt | ❌ TAKEN | Well-funded NY fintech/FP&A platform ($17M raised, YC). liveflow.io |
| **Linkway** | Messaging | ❌ TAKEN | Gulf IT/ERP company, 700+ clients, apps on Play Store |
| **Vaultline** | Messaging (secure) | ❌ TAKEN | Multiple: fintech/trading, e-banking, hosting, an ISP. Crowded space |
| **Cipher / Cipherway** | Messaging (secure) | ⚠️ RISKY | "Cipher" = major global cybersecurity MSSP + CipherChat encrypted messenger (cipherchat.co) + Cipher Security. Too close — high confusion risk |
| **Enclave** | Messaging (secure) | ❌ TAKEN / GENERIC | Enclave.io (networking), Enclave AI, plus a generic security term |
| **Obscura** | Messaging (secure) | ❌ TAKEN | obscura.now — E2E encrypted, wallet-native messenger |
| **Cloaq** | Messaging (secure) | ❌ TAKEN | cloaq.app — secure AI platform; also an older anonymous social app |
| **Haven** | Messaging (secure) | ❌ TAKEN | havenmessenger.com — existing secure messenger |

### Key takeaway
The **privacy/security naming space is saturated** — vault, cipher, cloak, shadow, enclave, obscura, haven are all in use. Dictionary words and obvious security metaphors are gone. The realistic path for the E2E Messenger is a **coined word** that *evokes* security without using the obvious roots.
